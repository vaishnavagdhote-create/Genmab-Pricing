Reimbursement launch tracker

HTA submission status, decision outcomes, and the critical gap between regulatory approval, HTA decision, and actual patient access. For a biomarker-selected IO, companion diagnostic reimbursement is a gating dependency.

Access ≠ HTA decision ≠ approval. These three dates differ in every EU market. A positive HTA opinion does not mean patients can access the drug — formulary listing, regional budget allocation, and companion diagnostic reimbursement each add lag. Model all three milestones separately.

Gross-to-net waterfall

The bridge from list price (WAC) to net realised price after all deductions. For high-price oncology, GTN erosion of 25–55% is typical. This is the price that actually triggers IRP and MFN mechanisms — not the list price.

Competitive landscape

In-class incumbent and pipeline threats. For a best-in-class entrant, the strategic question is where competitive displacement is winnable versus where the incumbent has locked-in contract, formulary, and prescriber advantages.

Displacement difficulty by market

Higher score = harder to displace the incumbent. Driven by incumbent's formulary entrenchment, confidential discount depth, and prescriber loyalty.
Best-in-class counter-positioning: In markets where the incumbent is most entrenched (deep rebates, positive HTA already issued), launching second with a differentiated value story often beats racing head-on. Sequence competitive displacement where it is winnable first.
